\subsection{Fine-Tuned Transformer Model (BERT-base-uncased)}
Overall, the fine-tuned BERT-base-uncased model had a relatively strong performance for the movie genre classification task. It significantly outperformed both baseline models by using contextual embeddings to capture complex semantic relationships in movie descriptions. 
Despite its strengths, it was still limited by factors such as class imbalance in the dataset and the inherent ambiguous and subjective nature of movie descriptions. This caused the model to perform very well on common genres within the dataset, and struggle with rarer labels. 
In sum, BERT is relatively strong and effective for this task and demonstrates the advantages of pretrained transformer models for natural language classification problems.
